Hate Crimes in America

A hate crime is a criminal offense against a person or property motivated in whole or in part by an offenderâ€™s bias against a race, religion, disability, sexual orientation, ethnicity, gender, or gender identity.
The FBI started tracking hate crime statistics starting in 1995. Entities such as cities, universities, and state counties are responsible for collecting and reporting hate crime data to the FBI each quarter. However, some states have very low percentages of jurisdictions reporting data, leading to an underrepresentation of hate crimes commited in that state and the US in general.

States outlined below have over 50%(by population) of their responsible entities reporting no data for the year 2017

States without any kind of hate crime protections: Arkansas, Georgia, Indiana, South Carolina, Wyoming

States with best reporting averages

New Mexico had 100% reporting in 2017

Washington DC also had 100% reporting in 2017, and the highest number of reported hate crimes per capita

Maryland and Virginia both had 89% of all entities reporting hate crime data

States with worst reporting averages

Hawaii Reports No Hate Crime Data to the Federal Government

New Hampshire and Rhode Island each had 74% of entities not reporting data.

Nevada had 71% of entities not reporting any data

Hate Crimes are on the rise since 2015. However, many states are reporting no data at all. This is troubling because the real scope of the problem cannot be acertained without better data. (Source: FBI)
